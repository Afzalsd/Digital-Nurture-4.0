Community News Bulletin

The City Council has approved the new community center renovation project, which will begin construction this fall. The $2.5 million project will include updated fitness facilities, a new computer lab, and expanded meeting spaces for local organizations.

Local resident Sarah Martinez has been recognized for her outstanding volunteer work with the neighborhood watch program. Her dedication to community safety has helped reduce local crime by 15% over the past year.

The annual block party planning committee is seeking volunteers to help organize this year's celebration. The event is scheduled for August 20th and will feature live music, food vendors, and activities for all ages.

New traffic lights have been installed at the intersection of Main Street and Oak Avenue following community requests for improved pedestrian safety. The lights are now operational and include audible crossing signals.

The community garden project has exceeded expectations with over 50 families participating in the program. Surplus vegetables are being donated to the local food bank, helping to address food insecurity in our area.
